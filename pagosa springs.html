Pagosa Springs

Pagosa Springs sits at 7,126 feet above sea level in the San Juan Mountains. The town is named by the Southwestern Ute word, "Pagosh" which means "healing waters." That's because Pagosa is home to the world's deepest geothermal hot spring aquifer. And visitors year after year visit to soak in these hot springs. There is also an annual hot air balloon festival that attracts many visitors to the area.
